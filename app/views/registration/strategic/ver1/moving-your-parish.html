1. Choose the right domain for your parish council

A domain is a unique address on the internet. All internet-based services such as websites, emails, video calling, document storage and mapping services need a domain to work.

Every domain is designed for a specific use. For example, there are many domains in use including:

  • .com for businesses
  • .co.uk for UK businesses
  • .org or .org.uk for non-profit organisations
  • .info for educational and informational purposes
  • .net.uk for internet service providers

Many parish councils wrongly use one of the above or similar domains for their websites instead of a .gov.uk domain, which was specifically created for use by central, devolved and local UK public sector organisations.

Almost all councils have also used free email services at some point, which is not recommended. Free email accounts do not look professional and some providers use email scanning for ad personalisation in exchange for providing services for free. These email accounts are also in control of the individuals using them and not under the control of the council.

CDDO recommends using .gov.uk domains for both council websites and emails.

2. Understand the benefits of a gov.uk domain for your parish council

Parish councils can get the benefits of using a gov.uk domain including having:

  • the trusted .gov.uk brand that people will recognise
  • improved rankings in search engines
  • continuous central government monitoring for potential security vulnerabilities
  • the ability to better control email accounts by removing staff when they leave
  • better legal protection because it's based in a UK jurisdiction
  • increased transparency and security compared to personal email accounts

Read more on the benefits of using a gov.uk domain.

3. Get help from the Parish Council Domain Helper Service

If applying for a .gov.uk domain is new to you, the Parish Council Domains Helper Service can help you through the process step-by-step. You can email parish-helper@domains.gov.uk to get started.

This service offers:

  • workshops to help build your knowledge so you can move to a .gov.uk domain
  • information about your role in securing the domain name as the registrant
  • how to maintain best working practices for the future
  • information about available funding

4. Understand your role as a registrant

As the clerk of the council you are the registrant. This role means you have control over the domain name and all related services, such as emails and the website.

The registrant is accountable for:

  • contacting a .gov.uk Approved Registrar to apply for and manage the domain name
  • overseeing and approving any modifications to the domain name
  • managing email accounts and the website
  • making sure the domain and its use comply with relevant guidelines and policies

5. Talk to your council

When you are ready to talk to your council about getting a .gov.uk domain you will need to:

  • Explain to your council members why they should move to a gov.uk domain.
  • Agree what services your council will use the domain for (website, email, other services).
  • Discuss your budget. For example, by discussing what services you want from the checklist.
  • Decide on your council's .gov.uk domain name. You can read more in the choosing an appropriate .gov.uk domain guidance.
  • Get council approval to apply for a .gov.uk domain name during your meetings

6. Check the services you need

When you understand the services you need, you can plan your budget and take advantage of the financial support available. This will help your council make a smooth and cost-effective move to a .gov.uk domain.

7. Tips for choosing a .gov.uk Approved Registrar

When your parish council has agreed to move to a gov.uk domain, you can start to look for a supplier who will register your new .gov.uk domain name. They are known as .gov.uk Approved Registrars.

  • Make sure that the Approved Registrar you are considering can actually provide the services, security, and support your council needs. Do not focus solely on cost.
  • Look for value in bundles. Registrars sometimes ‘bundle’ services together at a reduced price. Do make sure any bundle you choose to buy includes only the services your council needs, or you could end up paying for something you do not use.
  • You will have a long-term working relationship with the Approved Registrar so it’s important you feel you are comfortable communicating with them. It can be as simple as feeling that they use language you easily understand and they have taken the time to reassure you and answer your questions.
  • Get at least 3 quotes to help make sure you are getting the best value. If you are not sure about who to choose, you can ask for recommendations from other parish councils or through forums such as the Facebook Community Clerks’ Network.
  • We have created a shortlist of .gov.uk Approved Registrars who have agreed to provide additional support to parish councils. When you choose one of the .gov.uk Approved Registrars from this shortlist, you can expect:
    • helpful support in non-technical language
    • the safe movement of your existing services to your new domain
    • the forwarding of emails to your new domain
    • a technical point of contact to address and promptly resolve problems
    • active support including telephone, online ticket or video call support
    • minimal disruption during the set up of your new domain and services
    • ongoing help so that your council complies with the 2024 JPAG Practitioners Guide

8. Questions to ask .gov.uk Approved Registrars

Before you approach an Approved Registrar in the shortlist, prepare questions about the services you need and any costs. You could ask the following:

  • What bundles do you provide and how much will it cost yearly?
  • What methods of support do you offer?
  • I would like to keep my existing website and move it to .gov.uk - what is involved?
  • Will you help me create an accessibility statement for my existing website?
  • Will you help get councillors and other staff set up with their email?
  • Will you fix technical issues on my behalf?
  • Will you help me renew my existing domain name to keep it safe?

9. Make sure your council has an IT policy in place

It's good practice for your council to have an IT policy in place for when you start to use your .gov.uk domain. Everyone working for the council should be aware of and comply with the IT policy.

Your IT policy can cover:

  • acceptable use of IT resources including devices and software
  • data management and security
  • network and internet use
  • appropriate use of email
  • good practice account security including passwords
  • staying secure when working with mobile devices and remotely
  • how to report security incidents

Download this IT policy template, which you can adapt for your parish council.

10. Follow the rules of using a .gov.uk domain name

Your .gov.uk Approved Registrar will usually manage the domain for you but it is good to familiarise yourself with the rules of using a .gov.uk domain name.
