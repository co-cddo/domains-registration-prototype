Choosing a .gov.uk Approved Registrar for your parish council

When your parish council has agreed to move to a gov.uk domain, you can start to look for a supplier who will register your new .gov.uk domain name. They are known as .gov.uk Approved Registrars.

We have created a shortlist of .gov.uk Approved Registrars who have agreed to provide additional support to parish councils. When you choose one of the .gov.uk Approved Registrars from this shortlist, you can expect:

  • helpful support in non-technical language
  • the safe movement of your existing services to your new domain
  • the forwarding of emails to your new domain
  • a technical point of contact to address and promptly resolve problems
  • a contact number for telephone support
  • minimal disruption during the set up of your new domain and services
  • ongoing help so that your council complies with the 2024 JPAG Practitioners Guide

Tips for selecting your .gov.uk Approved Registrar

You will have a long-term working relationship with the Approved Registrar so it's important you feel you are comfortable communicating with them.

It can be as simple as feeling that they use language you easily understand and they have taken the time to reassure you and answer your questions.

Check more than just the cost

Do not focus solely on cost. Make sure that the Approved Registrar you are considering can actually provide the services, security, and support your council needs.

Look for value in bundles

Registrars sometimes offer to ‘bundle’ services together at a reduced price.

Ask the .gov.uk Approved Registrars if they offer service bundles that could be good value for your council.

Do make sure any bundle you choose to buy includes only the services your council needs, or you could end up paying for something you do not use.

Pick three registrars to get quotes from

Look through the shortlist of .gov.uk Approved Registrars to find one that suits your council’s needs. Before you approach an Approved Registrar prepare questions about the services you need and any costs. You could ask the following:

  • What bundles do you provide and how much will it cost yearly?
  • What methods of support do you offer?
  • I would like to keep my existing website and move it to .gov.uk - what is involved?
  • Will you help me create an accessibility statement for my existing website?
  • Will you help get councillors and other staff set up with their email?
  • Will you fix technical issues on my behalf?
  • Will you help me renew my existing domain name to keep it safe?

Get help from the Parish Council Domains Helper Service

If you are unsure about what your chosen registrar has suggested to you, you can email parish-helper@domains.gov.uk to help you check if it suits your council’s needs.

Your council's IT policy

It's good practice for your council to have an IT policy in place for when you start to use your .gov.uk domain. Everyone working for the council should be aware of and comply with the IT policy.

Your IT policy can cover:

  • acceptable use of IT resources including devices and software
  • data management and security
  • network and internet use
  • appropriate use of email
  • good practice account security including passwords
  • staying secure when working with mobile devices and remotely
  • how to report security incidents

Download this IT policy template, which you can adapt for your parish council.

What to do when your .gov.uk domain is live

It takes an average of 30 days to get set up with a .gov.uk domain when you apply to get the funding.

Your .gov.uk Approved Registrar will usually manage the domain for you but it is good to familiarise yourself with how you are accountable for the .gov.uk domain name and what you can use it for.

Read our guidance on getting started with your .gov.uk domain for more information.

Contact

Email the Parish Council Domains Helper Service at parish-helper-service@domains.gov.uk if you have any questions or concerns about moving to a .gov.uk domain.
