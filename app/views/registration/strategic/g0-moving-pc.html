Guidance

Moving your parish council to a .gov.uk domain

This guide is for parish council clerks who want to set up or move their council to a .gov.uk domain.

From: Central Digital and Data Office and Department for Science, Innovation and Technology
Published 9 November 2024
Last updated 10 December 2024 — See all updates

The .gov.uk domain was specifically created for use by central, devolved and local UK public sector organisations. Parish councils are eligible to use the .gov.uk domain name for websites and emails.

Your first step is to get council approval to use a .gov.uk domain name. If you’ve already done this, you can start at step 2 - Check the services you need.

1. Get council approval to buy a .gov.uk domain

Get council approval to apply for a .gov.uk domain name during one of your official meetings so that it is recorded in your minutes.

Explain the benefits of a .gov.uk domain name to your council

Parish councils can get the benefits of using a gov.uk domain including having:

  • .com for businesses
  • .co.uk for UK businesses
  • .org or .org.uk for non-profit organisations
  • .info for educational and informational purposes
  • .net.uk for internet service providers

Many parish councils wrongly use one of the above or similar domains for their websites instead of a .gov.uk domain, which was specifically created for use by central, devolved and local UK public sector organisations.

Almost all councils have also used free email services at some point, which is not recommended. Free email accounts do not look professional and some providers use email scanning for ad personalisation in exchange for providing services for free. These email accounts are also in control of the individuals using them and not under the control of the council.

CDDO recommends using .gov.uk domains for both council websites and emails.

  • the trusted and professional .gov.uk brand that people will recognise
  • increased transparency and security compared to personal email accounts
  • the ability to manage email accounts by removing staff when they leave
  • easy access to staff email accounts to fulfil FOI obligations
  • continuous central government monitoring for potential security vulnerabilities
  • better legal protection because it’s based in a UK jurisdiction

All permanent staff and councillors must use individual .gov.uk email accounts rather than free personal email accounts to improve professionalism, maintain transparency and to increase security within the parish council sector.

Read more on the benefits of using a gov.uk domain.

2. Check the services you need

You will need to buy a domain, web hosting, email accounts and possibly get a new website. The costs will vary depending on the services you are buying, the size of your council and which .gov.uk Approved Registrar you choose. For example, the more councillors you have the more email accounts you will need to buy.

When you understand the services you need, you can plan your budget. This will help your council make a smooth and cost-effective move to a .gov.uk domain.

3. Understand your role as a registrant

As the clerk of the parish council you are the registrant. This role means you have control over the domain name and all related services, such as emails and the website. The supplier who registers and manages your gov.uk domain is called a .gov.uk Approved Registrar.

As the registrant you are accountable for:

  • contacting a registrar to apply for and manage your domain name
  • making sure you follow the rules for using a .gov.uk domain name

When your parish council has agreed to move to a gov.uk domain, you can start to look for an Approved Registrar to work with and send the application for you.

Email support@domains.gov.uk if you have any questions about being a registrant.

4. Choose a .gov.uk Approved Registrar

There are 199 .gov.uk Approved Registrars in the marketplace. CDDO has created a shortlist of registrars who have agreed to provide extra support to parish councils.

What to expect from shortlisted .gov.uk Approved Registrars

When you choose one of the .gov.uk Approved Registrars from this shortlist, you can expect:

  • helpful support in non-technical language
  • the safe movement of your existing services to your new domain
  • the forwarding of emails to your new domain
  • a technical point of contact to address and promptly resolve problems
  • active support including telephone, online ticket or video call support
  • minimal disruption during the set up of your new domain and services
  • ongoing help so that your council complies with the 2024 JPAG Practitioners Guide

Your .gov.uk Approved Registrar will manage the technical aspects of the domain for you. You will have a long-term working relationship with the registrar you use, so it’s important you feel you are comfortable communicating with them.

Choose an Approved Registrar from the shortlist

5. What to do when you've set up your domain

Start using your domain

Your .gov.uk Approved Registrar will usually manage the domain for you but it is good to familiarise yourself with guidance on getting started with your .gov.uk domain name

It's also good practice to test emails and your website to make sure everything is working as expected before sending out any launch communications to your users.

Get staff to sign an IT policy

To support you in managing your .gov.uk domain and services, it’s good practice for your council to have an IT policy in place. Everyone working for the council should be aware of and comply with the IT policy.

Your IT policy can cover:

  • acceptable use of IT resources including devices and software
  • data management and security
  • network and internet use
  • appropriate use of email
  • good practice account security including passwords
  • staying secure when working with mobile devices and remotely
  • how to report security incidents

Download this template IT policy and adapt it for your parish council.

Who to contact if you need help

In most cases, the .gov.uk Approved Registrars on the shortlist will be able to answer your questions and guide you through the process of setting up and using your .gov.uk domain name.

However, you can email parish-helper-service@domains.gov.uk if you need any impartial advice.
