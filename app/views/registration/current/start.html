The Cyber and Domains Protection team is responsible for approving all .gov.uk domain names. We have built this service to increase the security of the approvals process and help with our identity checks.

We will continuously improve the service.

Who can apply for approval

Use this service if you're a .gov.uk Approved Registrar and you want to apply for a .gov.uk domain name for a registrant.

{{ govukInsetText({ html: 'If you are a registrant, you must choose a .gov.uk Approved Registrar and pay them to make this application on your behalf.' }) }}

How long it takes to get approval

The approval process should take approximately 5 working days. We may ask for more information during this time and this may delay the process. Our processing times will also depend on the volume and complexity of applications currently in progress.

Before you start

You will need to:

  • check your registrant's organisation can get a .gov.uk domain name
  • check the .gov.uk domain name meets the domain name rules
  • check the .gov.uk domain name is available using the Nominet lookup tool or or EPP check command
  • provide the name, organisation and existing contact details of your registrant for us to identify them

For parish, town and community council applications you will also be asked to provide evidence that the clerk is currently in the role.

For central government organisation or arm's-length body (ALB) applications you will also be asked to provide proof your registrant has:

  • permission to apply for a .gov.uk domain name
  • an exemption from using the GOV.UK website
