Privacy policy: how we use your data

Who we are

The Cyber and Domains Protection team at the Government Digital Services (GDS) is responsible for approving all .gov.uk domain names on behalf of the Cabinet Office.

The Get approval to use a .gov.uk domain name is a service that lets eligible public sector organisations apply to use a .gov.uk domain name.

There are three parties involved in the registration of a .gov.uk domain name:

  • Registrants who wish to apply for a domain name are data controllers.
  • The .gov.uk Approved Registrars who submit domain applications on behalf of registrants are data processors.
  • GDS, which processes applications and is a data controller.

The Cyber and Domains Protection team privacy notice

The Get approval to use a .gov.uk domain name service is part of a wider suite of services run by the CDDO Protecting Public Sector Domains Team.

You can read the full Cyber and Domains Protection team privacy notice here.
