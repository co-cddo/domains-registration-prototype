Apply for a .gov.uk domain name: step by step

This step-by-step will help public sector organisations check if they can get a .gov.uk domain name: what they need to do to apply, use and manage .gov.uk domains.

These pages form part of the Cabinet Office domain name registration and management rules. Within the Cabinet Office, the Protecting Public Sector Domains Team is responsible for approving and monitoring .gov.uk domains.

  1. Step 1: Check if your organisation can apply

    1. Benefits of getting a .gov.uk domain
    2. Check your organisation can get a .gov.uk domain name
    3. Get permission to apply for a .gov.uk domain name

  2. Step 2: Identify a registrant and choose a .gov.uk Approved Registrar

    All organisations eligible for a .gov.uk domain name must identify a registrant who is going to be responsible for the domain and then choose a .gov.uk Approved Registrar.

    1. Identify a .gov.uk registrant for your .gov.uk domain name
    2. Choose a .gov.uk Approved Registrar

  3. Step 3: Choose your .gov.uk domain name

    1. Choose an appropriate .gov.uk domain name

  4. Step 4: Get approval to use a .gov.uk domain name

    Registrants must choose a .gov.uk Approved Registrar, who will apply for the .gov.uk domain name on their behalf.

    1. Download the list of Approved Registrars.

  5. Step 5: Use your .gov.uk domain name

    Registrants must follow the Cabinet Office guidelines once their .gov.uk domain is live, or CDDO will withdraw it. For example, registrants must keep contact details up to date.

    1. Get started with your .gov.uk domain name
    2. Use and protect your .gov.uk domain name properly
    3. Keep your .gov.uk domain name secure

  6. Step 6: Manage your .gov.uk domain name

    You can make changes to your .gov.uk domain name.

    1. Renew your .gov.uk domain name
    2. Modify or transfer your .gov.uk domain name

    If your organisation no longer needs a .gov.uk domain name, you must take steps to protect it.

    1. Stop using your .gov.uk domain name
    2. What to do if your .gov.uk domain name is compromised
